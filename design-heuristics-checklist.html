Nielsen’s 10 heuristics (quick table)

HeuristicMeaningExamples
Visibility of system statusKeep users informed about what’s going on through timely feedback.Loading indicators, progress bars, success/error toasts
Match between system and the real worldUse language and concepts familiar to users; follow real-world conventions.“Cart” vs “Order Container”; human-friendly dates and labels
User control and freedomSupport undo/redo, back, and cancel so users can recover from mistakes.Undo action, Cancel upload, Back to previous step
Consistency and standardsFollow platform conventions and keep patterns consistent across the product.Same button styles, same terminology, predictable layouts
Error preventionPrevent problems before they happen rather than only displaying errors after.Disable invalid actions, input constraints, confirmations for destructive actions
Recognition rather than recallMinimize memory load by making options visible and information retrievable.Autocomplete, recent items, inline hints, clear menus
Flexibility and efficiency of useEnable shortcuts and accelerators for experienced users while remaining friendly to novices.Keyboard shortcuts, bulk actions, power filters
Aesthetic and minimalist designRemove irrelevant information; keep the UI focused and readable.Reduce visual noise, clear hierarchy, one primary CTA
Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errorsError messages should explain what happened and how to fix it.Field-level errors, actionable guidance, recovery steps
Help and documentationProvide help when needed, ideally contextual and searchable.Tooltips, empty-state guidance, help center links

Source: Nielsen Norman Group – 10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design ↗

Actionable checks (paste into reviews/tickets)

Use these as a practical checklist by module:

Feedback & status

  • Is there a clear loading/saving/submitting state?
  • Does the UI confirm success/failure after actions?
  • For long-running tasks: progress, cancel, and/or background notifications?

Content & IA

  • Does the page title clearly reflect the content?
  • Are primary buttons action-oriented (Save, Submit, Create)?
  • Is the same concept labeled consistently across screens?

Forms & inputs

  • Are required fields clearly indicated?
  • Are errors shown near the field with cause + fix?
  • Are defaults, hints, and autocomplete used appropriately?

Navigation & control

  • Can users go back / cancel / undo where appropriate?
  • Are destructive actions confirmed (delete, revoke access, pay)?
  • Does it follow platform conventions (Web/iOS/Android)?

Consistency & accessibility

  • Are components and spacing consistent?
  • Is contrast sufficient and keyboard focus visible?
  • Do icons have labels/tooltips when meaning isn’t obvious?

AI UI add-ons

  • Does the AI output show uncertainty or sources when relevant?
  • Are there controls like stop, retry, edit, regenerate?
  • Is history available and outputs easy to copy/export?
