Reading Sample

Wouldn't it be great if we could enter a key phrase and pull up information we stored in our brain? Sadly, that isn't how this brain of ours works. When it comes to retention, there are two important parts of our brain: working memory, that part where we think, see, feel, and hear information. The second part is where we store that information, our database, if you will.

When you forget a name given only moments before, it isn't really lost; you just can't find it. We depend on a neural link between our working memory and our storage, and it is very weak at the start. We must build it into a chain if we want to pull up that name whenever we need it.

The capacity of the brain is so massive that its capacity is estimated to be the equivalent of 500 years of movies. That is a lot of data, so it should not surprise us that information can get lost in there. The initial neural link is so flimsy that the name of that new person, we were introduced to just 30 seconds ago, could already be lost.

Research Goals

This typography accessibility research evaluates the optimal paragraph reading experience for KeelLearn lessons across desktop and mobile devices.

Research Scope
  • Compare font-size, line-height, and CPL (characters per line)
  • Test browser zoom levels at 100%, 125%, 150%, and 200%
  • Evaluate scanning rhythm and paragraph tracking
  • Measure focus retention and eye fatigue during long-form reading
  • Compare desktop and mobile readability accessibility
  • Identify the most balanced typography system for lesson content

✅Option 1 is the Best Overall Choice

Option 1 (16px / 26px / #313131) remains the recommended typography system for KeelLearn lesson content based on comparative readability testing, browser zoom scalability, accessibility behavior, scanning efficiency, and long-form learning readability across desktop and mobile devices.

  • 16px font-size maintains strong readability while scaling more effectively at higher browser zoom levels (150%–200%).
  • 26px line-height provides balanced paragraph spacing, efficient scanning rhythm, and stable reading density for learning-focused content.
  • Better zoom scalability than larger editorial-style typography systems such as 18px / 32px and 20px / 36px.
  • Maintains stronger information density and reduces excessive scrolling during zoomed reading conditions
  • #313131 font color provides strong readability contrast without the harshness of pure black, reducing visual vibration and eye strain
  • WCAG accessibility note: WCAG 2.1 text-spacing guidance recommends that content remains functional and readable when users increase line-height to at least 1.5× the font-size. Option 1 already satisfies this accessibility guidance while maintaining stable reading behavior across browser zoom levels.

💡 Conclusion

Based on comparative accessibility and readability testing, the recommendation is to keep the current 16px / 26px typography system for KeelLearn lesson content without increasing the default paragraph font-size or line-height.
